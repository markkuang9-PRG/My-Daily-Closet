# Commercial Licensing

My Daily Closet is publicly distributed under **AGPL-3.0-only**.

That means the public repository is open source, but the project owner may also offer separate commercial terms for organizations that need rights or operational flexibility outside the AGPL.

## Why a commercial license may be needed

You may need a separate commercial agreement if you want:

- private distribution terms outside AGPL obligations;
- proprietary integrations or redistribution rights;
- OEM, white-label, or embedded product terms;
- enterprise support, warranties, indemnities, or negotiated service commitments;
- trademark or branding rights that are not granted under the repository license.

## Open source vs commercial terms

- The public repository remains governed by [LICENSE](LICENSE).
- Contributor submissions are accepted under [CLA.md](CLA.md), which helps preserve the project's ability to offer commercial terms in the future.
- Names, logos, and branding remain subject to [TRADEMARKS.md](TRADEMARKS.md).

## How to start a conversation

For private commercial licensing, partnership, OEM, acquisition, or diligence discussions, contact the project owner privately through GitHub rather than opening a public issue.

When reaching out, include:

- your company or project name;
- intended use case;
- deployment model;
- whether you need support, trademark rights, or private licensing terms;
- any relevant timeline.
